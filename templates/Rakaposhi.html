The Rakaposhi

Welcome to the Rakaposhi Peak

Rakaposhi Peak, standing at 7,788 meters, is the 27th highest mountain in the world and is renowned for its stunning beauty and dramatic rise over the surrounding terrain. Located in the Karakoram range of Pakistan, it offers breathtaking views and is a popular destination for trekkers and climbers alike. Here are some key details about Rakaposhi Peak

Location and Name

  • Rakaposhi Peak is located in the Bagrote Valley of Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan.
  • The name "Rakaposhi" is derived from the local language, meaning "Snow Covered" or "Shining Wall".

    • Height and Climbing History

  • Rakaposhi Peak stands at an elevation of 7,788 meters (25,551 feet).
  • The first successful ascent of Rakaposhi Peak was made in 1958 by a British-Pakistani expedition led by Mike Banks and Tom Patey.

    • Geography and Climate

  • Rakaposhi Peak is known for its steep rise over the surrounding terrain, with an elevation gain of over 5,800 meters (19,000 feet) in just 11 kilometers.
  • The climate around Rakaposhi Peak is characterized by cold temperatures and heavy snowfall, making it a challenging climb.

    • Popular Trekking Routes

  • The trek to the base camp of Rakaposhi Peak is a popular route for trekkers, offering stunning views of the mountain and surrounding valleys.
  • The trek to the base camp typically takes 3-4 days and is suitable for trekkers of all skill levels.

    • Visiting Rakaposhi Peak

  • Accessible via the Bagrote Highway, with the nearest major town being Gilgit.
  • Travelers can reach Bagrote village by a 45 mint drive from Gilgit.
